I am Agata Woldan-Lopez, a licensed social worker with a passion for helping patients in Illinois and New York.

If you feel stressed, have been experiencing a low mood for a long time, struggle with low self-esteem, anxiety, loss, grief, a chronic illness—either your own or that of a loved one—feel burned out, or find your relationships with those around you have become difficult, don’t give up on yourself. Help is within reach. I invite you to work together on finding a path to a more conscious and fulfilling life.

I have many years of experience in therapy for anxiety disorders, panic attacks, and depression. I specialize in working with individuals who have experienced violence, have difficulty forming relationships, have gone through trauma, loss, or the death of a loved one, as well as those struggling to adapt to a new life situation, including emigration. My practice also includes support for transgender individuals and the entire LGBTQ+ community.

During therapy, we will explore your strengths, resilience, and adaptive abilities together. I will help you express your emotions, regain a sense of control over your life, and reconnect with yourself. Therapy will be tailored to your individual needs and pace. Don’t postpone your search for peace and fulfillment—schedule a free consultation.

Psychotherapy is a unique method of treatment and transformation in the areas of mental health, well-being, behavior, and personality functioning. Its goals focus on strengthening self-control, coping with stress, boosting self-esteem, and developing the ability to build relationships, cooperate, and communicate with others. Therapy often emphasizes deepening self-awareness and personal growth, which in turn helps to live more consciously.
